European Hydrotherapy

Charcot Shower

A powerful European technique using alternating high-pressure hot and cold water streams directed at the body from a distance — stimulating circulation, breaking down cellulite, and invigorating the entire nervous system.

15 / 25 / 40 min From $45

How It Works

The Charcot shower — named after the French neurologist Jean-Martin Charcot — directs a high-pressure water jet at your body from 3 to 5 meters away. The therapist controls the stream temperature and pressure, alternating between warm and cool water to create a powerful vascular workout for your circulatory system.

The alternating temperatures cause blood vessels to rapidly dilate and constrict, dramatically improving circulation and lymphatic flow. The water pressure acts as a deep tissue massage, reaching areas that are difficult to address with traditional techniques.

What to Expect

1

Warm-Up Phase

The session begins with a warm, fan-shaped spray across your body to relax muscles and open pores. Your therapist adjusts pressure to your comfort level.

2

Contrast Therapy

Alternating streams of warm (38-40°C) and cool (18-20°C) water target specific body areas — legs, hips, back, and arms — in systematic sweeping patterns.

3

Cool Down

A gentle warm rinse followed by light towel-drying. Many clients report an immediate sense of lightness, energy, and tingling warmth across the skin.
